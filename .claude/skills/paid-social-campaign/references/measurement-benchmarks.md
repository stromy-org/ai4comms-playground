# Measurement & Benchmarks Reference

Paid social benchmarks and measurement guidance for B2B campaigns.

## Important Caveat

**Benchmarks vary wildly.** Industry, geography, audience size, creative quality, offer strength, and campaign maturity all affect performance. The ranges below are directional only — derived from aggregated B2B campaign data across multiple sources and time periods. They are starting points for setting expectations, not targets to optimize against.

**Your own account history is always more reliable than industry benchmarks.** After 2-4 weeks of campaign data, use your own numbers as the baseline.

## How to Interpret Benchmarks

### Your Account History Beats Industry Data
- After 2-4 weeks of live campaigns, your own performance data is the most relevant benchmark.
- Track week-over-week trends within your account rather than comparing to external benchmarks.
- If your CPL is $80 on LinkedIn but your lead-to-customer conversion rate is 15%, that may be more efficient than a $30 CPL with 2% conversion.

### Platform Metrics vs. Business Metrics
- Platform metrics (CTR, CPC, CPL) measure advertising efficiency.
- Business metrics (MQL rate, pipeline generated, cost per opportunity, customer acquisition cost) measure business impact.
- Optimize for business metrics. Platform metrics are leading indicators, not goals.

### The CPL Trap
- A low CPL on Meta ($20) that produces 5% MQL rate yields an effective cost-per-MQL of $400.
- A high CPL on LinkedIn ($80) that produces 20% MQL rate yields an effective cost-per-MQL of $400.
- Same outcome, different story at the top of the funnel. Always trace through to pipeline.

## What "Good" Looks Like in B2B Paid Social

### Leading Indicators (first 2-4 weeks)
- Campaigns exit learning phase and stabilize.
- CTR is within or above benchmark ranges for the platform.
- CPL is declining week over week as algorithms optimize.
- Lead volume is sufficient for sales team to qualify (minimum 10-20 leads/week for meaningful feedback).

### Lagging Indicators (4-12 weeks)
- MQL rate: 10-25% of form fills become marketing-qualified leads (LinkedIn). 5-15% (Meta).
- SQL rate: 20-40% of MQLs become sales-qualified (varies by sales process).
- Pipeline influenced: Attributable pipeline value from paid social campaigns.
- Cost per opportunity: Total spend divided by opportunities created. The metric that matters most.

### Mature Program Indicators (3-6 months)
- Attribution model accounts for multi-touch influence (not just last-click).
- Retargeting campaigns are consistently lower cost and higher conversion than prospecting.
- Creative refresh cadence is established (every 4-8 weeks) with A/B testing generating actionable insights.
- CRM feedback loop is working: lead quality data flows back to campaign optimization.

## Common Measurement Pitfalls

### 1. Optimizing for CPL Without Checking Lead Quality
- **Problem**: Platform algorithms optimize for the cheapest conversions. Cheap leads often come from less qualified audiences.
- **Fix**: Track MQL and SQL rates by campaign. Increase CPL tolerance for campaigns that produce higher-quality leads.

### 2. Ignoring Attribution Windows
- **Problem**: Different platforms use different default attribution windows. LinkedIn 30-day click, Meta 7-day click + 1-day view, Google varies.
- **Fix**: Standardize attribution windows across platforms when possible. Use 7-day click as a common baseline for comparison. Note when view-through conversions inflate numbers.

### 3. Double-Counting Conversions
- **Problem**: A user clicks a LinkedIn ad, then later clicks a Meta ad, then converts. Both platforms claim the conversion.
- **Fix**: Use a single source of truth (GA4 or CRM) with consistent attribution model for cross-platform comparison. Platform reporting is for platform optimization; CRM reporting is for business performance.

### 4. Measuring Too Early
- **Problem**: Judging campaign performance during the learning phase (first 1-2 weeks) when costs are volatile and algorithms haven't optimized.
- **Fix**: Set a minimum evaluation period (2-4 weeks) before making performance judgments. Track trends, not snapshots.

### 5. Ignoring Incrementality
- **Problem**: Retargeting campaigns show amazing metrics because they're reaching people who were already going to convert.
- **Fix**: Periodically run holdout tests (exclude a portion of retargeting audience) to measure true incremental lift from paid social.

### 6. Vanity Metrics Masquerading as KPIs
- **Problem**: Reporting impressions, reach, and engagement as success metrics when the business goal is pipeline.
- **Fix**: Report platform metrics as context. Lead with business metrics: leads generated, MQLs, pipeline influenced, cost per opportunity.
